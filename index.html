State Solar Rebates & Local Incentive Programs

Many states offer extra solar rebates or performance-based incentives on top of the 30% federal solar tax credit. These include state tax credits, direct rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Certificates (SRECs), property tax exemptions, and sales tax exemptions on solar equipment. Solar Financing: PPA vs. Loan vs. Cash — What Saves You the Most?

Power purchase agreements (PPAs), leases, and solar loans each change who owns the system and how you pay. Cash purchases offer the highest total savings. Solar loans let you own the system and claim the 30% tax credit while paying over time. Leases and PPAs require $0 down but provide lower total savings. Frequently Asked Questions About Solar Energy

What is the federal solar tax credit?

The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets homeowners deduct 30% of their total solar installation cost from federal income taxes. This includes panels, inverters, batteries, labor, and permitting costs. The credit is available through 2032 at 30%, then drops to 26% in 2033.

How much can I save by going solar?

Most homeowners save between $20,000 and $60,000 over the 25-year lifespan of their solar system. Savings depend on your local electricity rates, sun hours, system size, and available incentives. Use our calculator to get a personalized estimate.

Do solar panels work in cloudy weather?

Yes. Solar panels still produce electricity on cloudy days, though at reduced output (typically 10-25% of full capacity). Modern panels are designed to capture diffuse sunlight. Germany, one of the cloudiest countries in Europe, is a global leader in solar adoption.
